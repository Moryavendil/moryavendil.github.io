abstract: A theory describing two-phase displacement in the gap between closely spaced
  planes is developed. The main assumptions of the theory are that the displaced fluid
  wets the walls, and that the capillary number Ca and the ratio of gap width to transverse
  characteristic length ε are both small. Relatively mild restrictions apply to the
  ratio M of viscosities of displacing to displaced fluids; in particular the theory
  holds for M = o(Ca−1/3). We formulate the theory as a double asymptotic expansion
  in the small parameters ε and Ca1/3. The expansion in ε is uniform while that in
  Ca1/3 is not, necessitating the use of matched asymptotic expansions. The previous
  work of Bretherton (1961) is clarified and extended, and both the form and the constants
  in the effective boundary condition of Chouke, van Meurs & van der Poel (1959) and
  of Saffman & Taylor (1958) are determined.
